What is BMI and Why Does It Matter? ⚖️

📅 June 23, 2026 • ⏱️ 4 min read • ⚖️ Health

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple measure that uses your height and weight to estimate body fat. It's one of the most commonly used tools to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight.

But what exactly is BMI, how is it calculated, and why does it matter? In this article, we'll break it all down in simple terms.

What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It's a number calculated from a person's weight and height. Doctors and health professionals use it as a quick screening tool to categorize people into weight categories.

How is BMI Calculated?

The formula is simple:

  • Metric: Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)²
  • Imperial: Weight (lbs) ÷ Height (in)² × 703

BMI Categories

BMI results fall into four main categories:

Category BMI Range Status
Underweight Below 18.5 ⚠️ Underweight
Normal 18.5 – 24.9 ✅ Normal
Overweight 25.0 – 29.9 ⚠️ Overweight
Obese 30.0 and above ❌ Obese

Why Does BMI Matter?

BMI is important because it helps identify potential health risks. Research shows that people with a BMI in the "normal" range tend to have lower risks for:

  • Heart disease
  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Certain cancers

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful tool, it has some limitations:

  • It doesn't distinguish between fat and muscle. Athletes may have a high BMI but low body fat.
  • It doesn't account for age, gender, or ethnicity.
  • It doesn't show where fat is stored (belly fat is more dangerous).

What Should You Do Next?
